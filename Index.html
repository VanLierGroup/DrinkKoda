Sleep architecture

How KN-01® supports
the full night.

KODA Night is formulated across four physiological targets — cortisol, GABA, magnesium, and the gut-immune axis — not just one mechanism.

1
Evening Cortisol Drop

Ashwagandha KSM-66's withanolides interact with the HPA axis to support the natural evening cortisol decline. Elevated evening cortisol is the primary driver of delayed sleep onset and reduced slow-wave sleep. KSM-66 at clinical doses demonstrates measurable cortisol reduction in peer-reviewed trials.

2
GABA Support via Magnesium

Magnesium is a cofactor for GABA production — the brain's primary inhibitory neurotransmitter. Glycinate chelation ensures the magnesium reaches neurological tissue rather than being sequestered in bone. The muscle-relaxing effect of magnesium glycinate is measurable within 30 minutes at 200mg doses.

3
Alpha-Wave Shift

L-Theanine at 200mg promotes alpha-wave EEG activity — associated with the alert-but-relaxed state that precedes sleep onset. Unlike sedatives, L-theanine does not induce drowsiness or impair next-day cognition. It creates the right neurological conditions for natural sleep initiation.

4
Immune Recovery During Rest

The gut-immune axis is most active during sleep. Reishi's beta-glucans interact with intestinal lymphoid tissue during the fasted, restorative state of sleep — the optimal window for immunomodulatory activity. Most adaptogenic benefits of Reishi compound over time; nightly dosing matches the biological rhythm of immune maintenance.
